StormHunters MUD


StormHunters is a MUD (a text-based online multiplayer game) that has been grown from a stock Ack!Mud 4.2 codebase into a completely different kind of world. Specific features were added to accommodate for the dwindling number of MUD players, as well as to encourage both cooperation and competition.

I took ownership of the game in 2007, and began overhauling features over the course of the next few years with a team of three. Work included C/C++ development, two iterations of websites and forums (one as a 24-hour challenge), feature roadmapping, market analysis, creative writing, world creation, and quality assurance.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Complete conversion of a flatfile saving system into an account-based system
  • Overhaul of skill and class system with over interconnected 200 abilities
  • Updated item customization system including pseudo-UI
  • Random quest generator to provide gameplay variety and retain player interest
  • Introduction of a gathering/crafting system inspired by other MMORPGs

BBM Meetings


BBM Meetings is a cross-platform solution that connects multiple participants in videoconferencing, regardless of device, operating system or locale.

I was responsible for working through the initial refresh of the BBM Meetings design document, adding on a large number of features and working through significant technical limitations. Approximately 8 iterations of the specification were delivered, each with multiple cross-departmental reviews being completed.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Development and maintenance of a 50+ page specification covering over 35 use cases
  • Multiple iterations to accommodate technical limitations due to cross-platform approach
  • Led discussions and reviews with internal and external product and development teams.

Blend Toolkit


BlackBerry Blend is an application that allows users to use and manage their smartphone content and applications from a computer or tablet. It acts as an extension of the smartphone and does not need to be on the same network to provide its functionality.

I was responsible for designing a companion toolkit that would migrate the majority of the BlackBerry Link device functionality to the Blend application. This included remote file access, software updates, de-brick support, and data backup.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Migrated functions and usage patterns from BlackBerry Link to Blend UI styling and flow
  • Participated in triage and descoping efforts to determine value of project goals
  • Aided in the design of an eventual transition to a co-existence between Blend and BlackBerry Link

BB10 UX Quality


BlackBerry 10 had a UX quality process known as "Bug Bashes", in which UX designers, development and product managers would simultaneously review common use cases and UI/UX of each product.

I was responsible for participation and coordination in these bug bashes, and was a lead reviewer for BlackBerry 10.2.1 and BlackBerry 10.3/Passport. This encompassed digging deep into an unfamiliar application, testing it from both an internal and external user POV, and ensuring that the implementation matched specifications and UX standards. Additionally, I was responsible for management of the JIRA Issue dashboard for UX, as well as preparing a weekly presentation of these results for my Senior Vice President to be shared with the COO and Quality Management.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Reviewed every application for UX quality and adherence to specifications for the BlackBerry Passport / 10.3 release, and over 10 applications for the 10.2.1 release.
  • Created a large number of JIRA issues during testing, ensuring a target timeline and that issue owners were identified
  • Maintained a JIRA dashboard to track down issues that were not progressing and drive them to closure
  • Crafted and presented a 10.2.1 value and improvements deck to senior carrier partners
  • Rolled up issue reports and progress for sharing by SVP with COO and company leadership

Device Switch


The Device Switch application was a utility that helped a user switch between BlackBerryOS devices, from BBOS to BB10, BB10 to BB10, and eventually iOS/Android to BB10.

I was the lead researcher on Device Switch during its BlackBerryOS inception. I created and maintained an interactive prototype for the entire flow of Bluetooth and Media Card Device Switch. This prototype served to capture plan of record, was used for usability sessions, and eventually used for QA.

I also provided guidance and design specifications for the BlackBerry10 Setup application, but this was replaced at a later time by the version supporting competitor platforms.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Lead researcher and designer for Bluetooth and Media Device Switch
  • Maintained interactive Axure prototype for all BT/SD flows
  • Conducted usability sessions exploring optimal paths for users
  • Reviewed progress and UI on a weekly basis with Senior VPs of Software, Design and Product

NFC & BlackBerry Tag


Near-Field Communication (NFC) is a short range wireless communication that was first available in BlackBerry 7.0 devices. It could be used for peer-to-peer file sharing, scanning static tags, and emulating a credit card.

I was the lead researcher on NFC during both the BlackBerryOS and PlayBook iterations. This involved a large number of device compatibility tests, paired usability sessions, survey research, and ergonomic investigations. My findings led directly to a number of revisions in the peer-to-peer sharing system.

For the PlayBook, I led usability investigations into NFC antennas, focusing on locations and interactions with smartphones and other devices.

Lastly, I continued to provide input and expertise into technical and planning documents for NFC on BlackBerry10 devices, like the Z10, and the Smart Tags application on all devices.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Lead researcher and design domain expert throughout BBOS and PlayBook
  • Ran a large number of paired usability sessions to tweak and define BlackBerry Tag
  • Provided input and recommendations directly to Senior VPs regarding go/no-go decisions
  • Maintained the specification document for the Smart Tags application

BlackBerry App World


BlackBerry App World was the first app storefront for BlackBerry devices. It consisted of both a client side, for purchasing, and a vendor side, for management.

I was responsible for conducting one-on-one usability sessions with application vendors, and analyzing the results. These results provided both guidance and prioritization to the Vendor Portal product team for their upcoming releases.

I also headed a usability effort designed to improve navigation and categorization of App World content. This involved crafting a specialized Excel spreadsheet that could be sent to all Beta users as an improvized mass card sort.

Lastly, I created a set of wireframes to demonstrate how a social wishlisting system could provide improvements over the upcoming Begging system, and how it would result in additional sales.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Facilitated usability sessions with vendors to assess upcoming changes to the Portal
  • Crafted a priority list and analysis of findings from usability sessions
  • Conducted a remote card sort with over 200 users using a specialized Excel tool
  • Produced a set of conceptual wireframes outlining a social wishlisting system

BB10 Connectivity

Balance - Corporate Liable


BlackBerry Balance was a compartmentalization system similar to user profiles that allows a company's IT department to fully manage and control work data on an employee's smartphone, without having access to their personal data.

Although I was involved with some of the original plans for BlackBerry Balance, I only took on design production work for the Corporate Liable scenarios. In these cases, data is still fully split into two compartments, but the IT group has access to both. This was primarily used for more security-minded companies, and those that were provisioning devices to employees at cost. Due to the new uses, there were a number of security-specific messages, application restrictions and activation paths that needed to be designed and communiated to development.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Provided insight into original requirements for BlackBerry Balance
  • Lead designer for Corporate Liable Balance
  • Delivered numerous iterations of a specification that bridged Product needs, UX, technical limitations and security requirements

Design DIY


The Design DIY program was set up after the initial release of BlackBerry10 to support a growing number of first-party applications and projects.

I co-led the DIY initiative with one of my colleagues, and our responsibilities ranged from internal presentations to full design review. I created and maintained tools for quick prototyping that was approachable for the less-design inclined (Powerpoint) and those more experienced (Inkscape open-source). Our goal was to ensure that no team felt bereft of UX support, and we guaranteed a basic response within 48 hours, and a full review within the week. I also acted as a key liaison between various teams and the Core designers, for any use cases that fell outside of our pattern guidance to date.


KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS

  • Evangelized the value of UX within the organization via presentations
  • Reviewed and provided guidance for over 15 applications
  • Maintained a 48-hour turnaround time to ensure that teams felt supported by Design/UX
  • Split up and tracked work using a specialized JIRA component
